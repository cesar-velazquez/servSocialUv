General information
Universidad Veracruzana Ir a uv.mx


Servicio Social  can be carried out in a period of 6 to 12 months, and you must cover a total of 480 hours of work in the place/institution/organization/enterprise/academic body where you will carry it out. We realise that 480 hours is a lot for one semester, so you may choose to complete your Servicio Social  over two semesters. Do be prepared to dedicate significant time to your Social Service project, but don’t be scared by it. It is recommended that those 480 hours are divided as follows:

  • Needs analysis.
  • Development of an action plan and project outline.
  • Preparation of lesson plans and materials.
200 hrs
  • Teaching of classes.
200 hrs
  • Discussing a lesson plan and observation by a colleague.
  • Self-evaluation and reflection in e-portfolio.
80 hrs

Total

480 hrs

In this course you are not going to be working alone: different relationships need to be made. First of all, we need to set up an agreement between institutions, i.e. The University of Veracruz and the receiving institution, organization or industry where you will render your Servicio Social  (unidad receptora). You will need to work with others such as your colleagues, your students and the authorities. And you should also maintain regular contact with your facilitator and classmates, both via your e-portfolio and by taking part in the regular forums and chat sessions that will be held.

Rationale

Rationale

There are some reasons to ask future professionals to carry out Social Service. We can look at them from different perspectives. Let’s begin with the legal one and then move to the formative one.

Social Service is mandatory in our country; it is fully justified and ruled for in our national Legislation from which other regulations derive, including the Universidad Veracruzana’s. Social Service began in Mexico in 1936. It was included in State Laws in 1939 as mandatory. Six months is the minimum duration. The national association of higher education institutions (ANUIES) created the national commission for Social Service in 1997.

However, in terms of education, Social Service is most important in its mission which is “to contribute to the integral development of the student, to develop their values and facilitate their insertion into the labor market besides contributing to solving contemporary development problems.” It is this side of Social Service that we intend to focus on in the BA in ELT.

Performance objectives

Performance objectives

On completing your Social Service, you should have reached the following objectives:

  • Complement your academic preparation as a future graduate (a principle of long-life learning).
  • Render SS as an integral part of the academic curriculum you are undertaking (a formative set of principles).
  • Help you as a student of a Higher Education Institution to articulate your learning within your teaching in a community (a principle of situated learning and teaching).
  • Benefit the least protected social sectors first (a principle of sustainability, situated teaching and learning and high human and professional values).

Methodology

Methodology

First, you should get acquainted to the whole guide so as to grasp its purpose and get some inspiration to prepare your project plan.

Then, the next essential step is to set up your own blog for your e-portfolio. There are many web services that offer free blog sites, Blogger and WordPress being among the most well-known. Whilst it is up to you to decide which service you use create your e-portfolio, Blogger is one of the simplest (and, therefore, easiest to use) options. Tutorial: Enlace How to create a blog in blogger. opens a new tab

It is your responsibility to find a receiving institution or organisation where you will render your Social Service. The receiving institution can be in the public, private or voluntary sector and at any level of education, but we ask for you to choose an institution which can offer you a placement that will provide you with teaching hours. Once you have found a receiving institution, the UV will send a letter, asking for the institution to support you and comply with certain responsibilities while you are rendering your Social Service there, including appointing an on-site coordinator for you.

When your placement has been formally confirmed, you can then begin your Social Service. There are a series of course activities that you will be asked to carry out during your placement, as well as some administrative duties. It is important that you keep a detailed record of everything that you do for your placement: this can be used for your e-portfolio, and will help you to fill in the monthly reports, which must be signed and stamped at your receiving institution before you send them to me.

We will also be keeping regular contact through the course forums and chat sessions. Remember, this is an opportunity to share!

Finding a placement

In order to begin your Servicio Social , you first need to find a receiving institution where you will carry out your service. Typically, this is done in a school or language institute. In Eminus, you will find a file (Receiving Units catalogue) in Important Documents with a list of institutions where students have done their placements in previous years. You may be interested in doing your placement in one of these institutions, or you might want to look for a different one (this will depend partly on where you are located). Please note that as of September 2015, there is a new law which requires official permission to be granted for placements in all SEP affiliated institutions at Pre-escolar  to Preparatoria  level. For those of you in the state of Veracruz, we can do the relevant paperwork for you here in Xalapa, once you have provided us with the full details of the institution.

To formalise your placement, an agreement needs to be formed between the Facultad de Idiomas  and the receiving institution. You will be sent a digital copy of a personalised letter to be handed in at your receiving institution, and you will need to ask the institution for a letter of acceptance in return (there is an example in the files attached). You will also need to fill out a registration form (directorio individual, attached), for our records.
